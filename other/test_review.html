Application of Differentiation Review Problems

Here are and handful of review problems I designed to ensure a solid understanding of how to apply derivatives.


1. Without a calculator, approximate \(1.1^2\cdot2\). Derermine whether your estimate is an over or under estamite. Show all work.

2. Bruce's water usage, in gallons, for a summer month lasting \(30\) days is defined by the function \(f(x)=\frac{1}{100}x^4-3x^2+5\) where \(x\) is time in days. If Bruce's city fines residents for using more than \(175\) gallons of water on any single day, should Bruce be fined?

3. Annabella buys \(50\) feet of fencing to make two square shaped pens. How much fencing should Annabella partition to each pen to maximize area?

4. Given the distance over time function \(f(t)=x^2+4x+2\), find the speed function. Then, use linear approximation to evaluate the speed function at \(t=-2.01\)

5. Reuben's gravitational potential energy (GPE) during a ferris wheel ride can be represented by the his weight (\(70 kg\)) times the force fo gravity (\(9.8 \frac{m}{s^2}\)). Reuben's height is represeneted by the function \(f(t)=125\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{10}t\right)+175\) where \(t\) is the number of minutes that have passed. If Reuben rides the ferris wheel for \(20\) minutes, after how many minutes does Reuben's GPE reach it's maximum?

6. A boat is reeled in on a winch 1 foot in diamter and 5 feet above the sea level. The winch is rotating at a rate of \(\frac{1}{2} \frac{ft}{s}\). When the boat is 12 feet (horizontally) from the winch, how fast is the boat being drawn in (horizontally).
